## 📊 Results

### 🔑 Key Insights

- **Urus is the core revenue driver**, leading both in volume and total revenue across all years  
- Sales grow steadily from **2020 to 2024**, reaching a peak, followed by a **-9% decline in 2025**  
- The **Americas is the top-performing market**, generating the highest share of total revenue  
- Sales growth slows over time:  
  - strong growth in early years (**+8% → +11%**)  
  - near stagnation in 2023 (**+2%**) → signal of market maturity  
- **Hybrid transition (2024)** significantly boosts performance:  
  - revenue increases by **+33% YoY**  
- **Revuelto outperforms expectations**, with 2024 sales exceeding **combined Aventador sales (2022–2023)**  
- **Customer preferences differ by segment**:  
  - Urus buyers prefer **black**  
  - supercar buyers (Revuelto, Aventador) prefer **white**  
- Most vehicles sold cluster around **~641 HP**, indicating a consistent performance sweet spot  

---

### 📈 Business Interpretation

- **Business heavily depends on the Urus** → concentration risk but also strong stability driver  
- The market shows **signs of saturation after 2022**, with declining growth rates  
- **Hybridization is a key growth lever**, but its success depends on product positioning  
- The **Revuelto demonstrates successful innovation**, combining lower price and hybrid tech  
- The **Temerario shows that not all innovation scales equally**, highlighting execution risk  
- Strong regional performance suggests **focus on high-revenue markets (Americas)**  

---

### 📅 Strategic Turning Point (2024)

- Product portfolio reshaped:
  - Aventador discontinued  
  - Revuelto introduced (hybrid flagship)  
  - Urus upgraded to hybrid (**Urus SE**, +21% price)  

**Result:**
- Major revenue acceleration (**+33% YoY**)  
- Clear shift toward hybrid technology  
- Strong market acceptance of new flagship  

---

### 📉 2025 Slowdown

- Sales decline **(-9% after peak year)**  
- New hybrid model (**Temerario**) fails to replicate Revuelto success  

**Implication:**
- Innovation alone is not sufficient  
- Product-market fit is critical  

---

### 🧠 Final Takeaways

- Growth is driven by a combination of:
  - **Urus dominance (volume + revenue stability)**  
  - **successful innovation (Revuelto)**  
  - **targeted hybrid transition**  

- However:
  - growth is **not uniform across models**  
  - the business shows **early signs of maturity**  
  - future performance depends on **execution of new product launches**
